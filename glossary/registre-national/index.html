Belgium Legal Term

Registre National

French/Dutch: Registre National / Rijksregister

Belgium's national population register assigning a unique 11-digit identification number (numéro de registre national / rijksregisternummer) to all residents. Required for almost all administrative and financial activities in Belgium.

TL;DRBelgium's national population register assigning a unique 11-digit identification number (numéro de registre national / rijksregisternummer) to all residents. Required for almost all administrative and financial activities in Belgium.

The Registre National (Rijksregister in Dutch) is Belgium's central database of all persons legally registered as residents in Belgium. Upon registering as a resident with a Belgian commune, every person receives a numéro de registre national (RRN / rijksregisternummer) — an 11-digit identifier derived from date of birth. This number becomes your primary administrative identity in Belgium, required for opening bank accounts, signing contracts, accessing public services, and filing tax returns.

EU citizens exercising free movement rights in Belgium must register with the local commune within 90 days of arrival. The commune verifies that you meet residency conditions (employment, self-employment, sufficient resources, or studies) and issues a certificate of registration (annexe 8 / bijlage 8 or E card for EU nationals). The RRN is assigned at this point.

Non-EU nationals require a valid visa and/or residence permit before registering. The process typically involves a visa application from the Belgian consulate in their home country, followed by registration at the commune upon arrival. The commune notifies the Immigration Office (Office des Étrangers / Dienst Vreemdelingenzaken), which issues the residence permit. Non-EU nationals receive a foreign nationals number (numéro d'étranger / vreemdelingennummer) if not entitled to full registration.

For Belgian property transactions, the RRN or vreemdelingennummer is required by the notaire for the acte authentique. If you are purchasing from outside Belgium and have not yet registered residency, your notaire will need your foreign identification documents. However, establishing Belgian residency — and obtaining an RRN — is required before you can benefit from the reduced registration duty rates (klein beschrijf in Flanders, abattement in Brussels/Wallonia).

Belgian banks require either an RRN or vreemdelingennummer to open an account, obtain a mortgage, or engage in any significant financial transaction. Expats purchasing Belgian property typically need to have their identification registered before the mortgage application process can begin.

⚠ Common MistakeAssuming you can benefit from Belgian primary residence registration duty reductions without actually transferring your registered residency. Tax authorities cross-reference Registre National data — if you claim primary residence rates but your RRN shows a different commune or you remain registered abroad, the authorities will reclaim the duty saving with penalties.
💡 Expert TipRegister at your Belgian commune as soon as possible after moving in, even before the acte authentique is signed (registering at a rental address first is common). Belgian registration is what grants you the RRN, which in turn unlocks banking, tax filing, and reduced property tax rates.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I have an RRN as a non-resident property owner?

No — the RRN is reserved for persons registered as Belgian residents. Non-resident property owners are assigned a BIS number (a temporary identifier used for tax purposes) rather than a full RRN. The BIS number allows them to file Belgian tax returns and interact with the Belgian tax authority without being counted as residents.

What is a BIS number and how do I get one?

A BIS number (also called numéro BIS) is assigned by the Belgian tax authority (SPF Finances) to non-residents who have taxable connections to Belgium (property ownership, Belgian-source income). Your notaire or Belgian accountant can request a BIS number on your behalf if you do not have an RRN.

Does registering in Belgium as a resident automatically make me Belgian tax resident?

Registration in the Registre National creates a strong presumption of Belgian tax residency. Belgium taxes residents on worldwide income. If you have registered residency in Belgium but also maintain tax residence elsewhere, a bilateral tax treaty determines which country has primary taxing rights — consult a cross-border tax specialist before registering.
