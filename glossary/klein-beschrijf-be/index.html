Klein Beschrijf

Klein beschrijf is Belgium's reduced registration duty rate of 3% for modest first homes, applying when the cadastral income of the property does not exceed a legally set threshold.

What Is Klein Beschrijf?

Klein beschrijf (literally 'small description') is a Belgian registration duty concession that reduces the rate from the standard 12.5% (Wallonia/Brussels) or 10% (Flanders — now abolished in favour of separate schemes) to 3% for qualifying first-home buyers of modest-value properties. The condition is that the property's kadastraal inkomen (cadastral income — a notional annual rental value set by the administration) does not exceed a statutory ceiling, which varies by region and family situation.

Flanders vs Wallonia vs Brussels

Belgium's three regions have diverging property tax regimes. In Flanders, klein beschrijf was effectively replaced by the abattement (tax-free tranche of €200,000) and later by a zero-rate scheme for the principal residence. In Wallonia and Brussels, klein beschrijf at 3% continues to apply to eligible purchases where the cadastral income falls below the threshold (approximately €745 base, uplifted for children). Buyers in each region must check their region's current rules — the concessions are not uniform.

Cadastral Income Threshold

The cadastral income threshold for klein beschrijf is increased for each dependent child: the base threshold rises by a fixed amount per child (€65 in Wallonia). This means larger families can benefit from the reduced rate on higher-value properties. The applicable cadastral income is the property's own cadastral income as recorded in the kadaster — not the buyer's income.

Conditions and Restrictions

To claim klein beschrijf, the buyer must: not have previously owned another property in Belgium (first-time buyer condition in most regions); intend to use the property as their primary residence within a defined period; and the property must meet the cadastral income ceiling. A buyer who has inherited a share of a property (e.g. an undivided fraction) may still qualify provided they did not hold full or majority ownership.

Tax Saving Impact

On a Brussels property at €300,000 with a modest cadastral income, the standard duty at 12.5% would be €37,500. At 3% (klein beschrijf), it drops to €9,000 — a saving of €28,500. This makes the eligibility check one of the first items any first-time Belgian buyer should discuss with their notary.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Buying a second property (even a small studio) before your primary home and thereby disqualifying yourself from klein beschrijf on the main purchase. Buy the primary home first.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Ask your notary to check the cadastral income of every property you view — the concession threshold is surprisingly high and many urban apartments qualify.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is klein beschrijf the same in all regions?

No — Flanders has different schemes; Wallonia and Brussels both use klein beschrijf but with different thresholds.

Does it apply to building land?

Klein beschrijf applies to residential buildings, not to bare building plots — land purchases are subject to the standard rate.

Can I claim it if I already own abroad?

Ownership of foreign property generally does not affect Belgian klein beschrijf eligibility — only Belgian property ownership is counted.
