Belgium Legal Term

Acte Authentique

French/Dutch: Acte Authentique / Authentieke Akte

The final notarised deed of sale in Belgium that legally transfers property ownership. Drafted by the notaire, read aloud, and signed by both parties. Ownership transfers at the moment of signing.

TL;DRThe final notarised deed of sale in Belgium that legally transfers property ownership. Drafted by the notaire, read aloud, and signed by both parties. Ownership transfers at the moment of signing.

The acte authentique (authentieke akte in Dutch) is the formal notarial deed that definitively transfers ownership of Belgian property. It is drafted by the notaire based on the compromis de vente and additional due diligence results, read aloud in full at the signing appointment, and signed by both buyer and seller (or their authorised representatives via procuration). Ownership transfers at the moment of signature.

The notaire plays a neutral public role — they represent neither buyer nor seller but the interests of both parties and of the state. The notaire verifies: the seller's title (titres de propriété), the absence of unresolved mortgages or charges (charges hypothécaires), planning compliance (conformité urbanistique), soil certificate status, and the identity and civil status of all parties. These checks protect the buyer but are not a substitute for independent legal advice.

Belgian notaire fees are set by a regulated tariff and are slightly lower than French notaire fees as a percentage of transaction value. Total acquisition costs in Belgium — including notaire fees, registration duties, and administrative charges — typically run to 15–18% of the purchase price for standard residential property at full registration duty rates, making Belgium one of the higher-cost property markets in Europe on an acquisition basis.

For expats who cannot attend the signing in person, a procuration (volmacht in Dutch) — a power of attorney — can authorise a trusted representative to sign on your behalf. This document must be in notarial form; if executed abroad, it typically requires apostille certification. Belgian notaires are experienced in handling international procurations and can advise on the exact format required for your nationality.

After signing, the notaire registers the acte with the Administration du Cadastre (Flanders: Vlaamse Belastingdienst) and the Mortgage Registry (Bureau des Hypothèques / Hypotheekkantoor). This registration makes the transfer public and effective against third parties. The process typically takes 3–6 weeks after signing.

⚠ Common MistakeAssuming the notaire has checked everything and no independent legal review is needed. The notaire verifies legal validity, not commercial fairness. Hire a separate Belgian avocat to review the compromis and advise on your specific situation — particularly regarding planning history, leases, and any rights affecting the property.
💡 Expert TipRequest a draft of the acte authentique from the notaire at least one week before signing. This is your legal right, and reviewing it carefully (ideally with a bilingual legal adviser) catches errors before they become legally binding. Pay particular attention to the property description, mortgage redemption amounts, and any conditions carried forward from the compromis.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I choose my own notaire in Belgium?

Yes — buyers are free to choose any Belgian notaire. If both parties use the same notaire, the fee is not split between two professionals; Belgian law allows a single notaire to act for both parties in a property transaction, though each party may also appoint their own at no additional total cost.

What happens to outstanding mortgages on the property I'm buying?

The notaire verifies all registered mortgages at the Mortgage Registry and deducts outstanding balances from the seller's sale proceeds at completion, paying the lenders directly. The buyer receives a clean title free from the seller's mortgage — but the notaire must confirm all mortgages have been identified and discharged.

Is the acte authentique publicly accessible?

The registration details (owner, encumbrances) are publicly accessible via the Belgian Mortgage Registry. The full text of the acte is held by the notaire and the Fonds des Archives Notariales, and can be obtained by parties to the deed and their successors. It is not freely public in the same way as some land registries.
